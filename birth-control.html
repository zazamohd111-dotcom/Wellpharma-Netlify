Birth Control Options We Can Prescribe

The Pill

How it works: Take one pill every day at the same time.

Success rate: 91% with typical use, 99% if you never miss a pill

Perfect for: People who like daily routines and want lighter periods

The Patch

How it works: Stick a new patch on your skin once a week for 3 weeks, then one week off.

Success rate: 91% with typical use

Perfect for: People who don't want to remember a daily pill

The Ring

How it works: Insert a small, flexible ring and leave it in for 3 weeks, then remove for one week.

Success rate: 91% with typical use

Perfect for: People who want to "set it and forget it"

The Shot (Depo-Provera)

How it works: Get an injection every 3 months.

Success rate: 94% with typical use

Perfect for: People who don't want to think about birth control often

Common Side Effects - What's Normal?

Most side effects are mild and go away after 2-3 months. Your body just needs time to adjust to the hormones.

Common side effects include:

  • Spotting between periods - Very common in the first few months
  • Nausea - Try taking the pill at bedtime with food
  • Tender breasts - Usually improves after a few weeks
  • Headaches - Often get better as your body adjusts
  • Mood changes - Tell us if this bothers you

The good news: Many people have NO side effects at all! And some side effects are actually benefits (like clearer skin and lighter periods).

When to call us: If side effects last more than 3 months or really bother you, we can help you switch to a different type.

Beyond Pregnancy Prevention: Other Benefits

While pharmacists prescribe birth control specifically for contraception, many people experience additional health benefits:

Your periods might get better:

  • Lighter bleeding - Less blood loss means less risk of anemia
  • Less cramping - Birth control reduces pain-causing chemicals
  • Regular cycles - Know exactly when your period is coming
  • Skip periods safely - For vacations or special events

Other health benefits many people experience:

  • Clearer skin - Especially with certain types of pills
  • Less PMS - Fewer mood swings and less bloating
  • Reduced cancer risk - Lower risk of ovarian and uterine cancer
  • Help with PCOS symptoms - Less unwanted hair growth and acne
  • Fewer menstrual migraines - Steady hormones mean fewer headaches

Remember: While these benefits are real, pharmacists in Maryland prescribe birth control specifically for pregnancy prevention. If you need treatment for other conditions, we'll help connect you with a doctor.

Who Can Get Birth Control from a Pharmacist?

You're a good candidate if you:

  • Are 18 or older (some pharmacies have different age policies)
  • Have normal blood pressure
  • Don't smoke (or are under 35 if you do)
  • Have no history of blood clots
  • Want to prevent pregnancy

You might need to see a doctor instead if you:

  • Have certain health conditions
  • Take medications that interact with birth control
  • Want birth control for reasons other than pregnancy prevention
  • Are under 18 (policies vary)

Don't worry - we'll help you figure out if pharmacy prescribing is right for you during your consultation!

Birth Control Myths - Busted!

❌ Myth: "Birth control makes you gain weight"
✅ Truth: Studies show the pill doesn't cause weight gain for most people

❌ Myth: "You need to take breaks from birth control"
✅ Truth: You can safely take birth control for years without breaks

❌ Myth: "Birth control causes infertility"
✅ Truth: Your fertility returns quickly after stopping birth control
