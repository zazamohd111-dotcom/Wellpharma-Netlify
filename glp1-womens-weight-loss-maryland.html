Our Services

Our Women's Weight-Management Services

We focus on helping women improve their weight, body composition, and energy through a structured, medication-supported program. All therapies are provided on a patient-specific prescription basis and used as part of a broader plan that includes nutrition guidance and movement recommendations.

GLP-1–Based Weight Management

Prescription-only GLP-1 therapy for women seeking clinically supported weight loss. Medications are dispensed on a patient-specific basis through our licensed compounding pharmacy.

Peptide Body-Composition Support

Sermorelin and peptide-based therapies to support lean muscle preservation, growth hormone optimization, and metabolic health as you lose weight.

Metabolic Nutrient Injections

Lipo-B and B12 combination injections to support energy levels, fat metabolism, and overall vitality throughout your weight-management program.

NAD+ Injections

NAD+ therapy for cellular energy support, recovery, and combating the fatigue that often accompanies active weight loss and caloric reduction.

Hormone (HRT) Consultation

Women's hormone consultations including ZRT lab testing to assess whether hormonal imbalances are affecting your weight, energy, metabolism, or mood.

Ongoing Monitoring & Support

Regular check-ins, adherence coaching, nutrition and movement guidance, and dose adjustments as directed by your prescribing clinician to keep you on track.

Medication Safety and Compounded Therapies

Some of the therapies used in our programs are FDA-approved medications. In certain situations, a compounded medication may be prescribed when a commercially available product does not meet a patient's specific clinical needs.

Compounded medications are prepared by licensed 503A compounding pharmacies on an individual, patient-specific basis. These preparations are not FDA-approved, which means they have not undergone FDA pre-market review for safety or effectiveness.

All medications are used under the direction of a prescribing clinician, and patients are monitored for response and tolerability throughout the program.
