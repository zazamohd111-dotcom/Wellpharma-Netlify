What Are GLP-1 Medications?

GLP-1 medications are the newest way to lose weight and control diabetes. The most popular ones are:

  • Ozempic and Wegovy (same medicine, different names)
  • Mounjaro and Zepbound (same medicine, different names)

These are weekly shots that help you:

  • Feel less hungry
  • Feel full faster
  • Lose 15-20% of your body weight (sometimes more!)
  • Control blood sugar better

Sounds great, right? They are! But there's something important you need to know.

The Hidden Challenge: Losing Muscle Along with Fat

Here's the truth: When you lose weight on GLP-1s, you don't just lose fat. You also lose muscle. Studies show that up to 40% of the weight you lose could be muscle mass. That's a big problem because muscle keeps you:

  • Strong and active
  • Able to burn calories
  • Independent as you age
  • Healthy overall

Think of it this way: losing fat is like cleaning out your garage. Losing muscle is like throwing away your tools. You need those tools!

Why GLP-1 Users Need Extra Nutrition

When you're on these medications, three things happen:

1. You Eat Much Less

The medicine makes you feel full quickly. That's how it helps you lose weight! But eating less means getting fewer vitamins and minerals from food.

2. Your Stomach Works Differently

GLP-1s slow down how fast food moves through your stomach. This can affect how well you absorb nutrients like vitamin B12 and iron.

3. Your Food Choices Change

Many people on GLP-1s don't want meat or protein-rich foods anymore. They prefer lighter foods. But protein is exactly what you need most!

Essential Supplements While on GLP-1s

Based on the latest research, here's what your body needs:

Protein - Your #1 Priority

Why it matters: Protein protects your muscles while you lose weight.

How much you need: At least 60-80 grams per day (more if you're bigger)

Easy ways to get it:

  • Protein shakes (20-30 grams per shake)
  • Greek yogurt (15-20 grams per cup)
  • Eggs (6 grams each)
  • Protein bars (10-20 grams each)

Tips for Success on GLP-1s

Eat Smart

  • Protein first at every meal
  • Small bites, chew well
  • Stop eating when you feel full
  • Stay hydrated (64 oz water daily)

Move Your Body

  • Walk 20-30 minutes daily
  • Lift light weights 2-3 times per week
  • Do squats and push-ups (even modified ones!)
  • Yoga or stretching helps too

Track Your Progress

  • Weekly weigh-ins (same day, same time)
  • Monthly body measurements
  • Photos every month
  • How you feel matters most!

Special Concerns for Older Adults

If you're over 60, muscle loss is extra risky. You need:

  • More protein (at least 1 gram per pound of ideal body weight)
  • Strength training (even chair exercises count!)
  • Regular testing for sarcopenia (muscle loss condition)
  • Careful monitoring by your pharmacist and doctor
