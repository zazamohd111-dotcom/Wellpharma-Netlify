Understanding Compounding

Pharmaceutical compounding is the art and science of preparing personalized medications for patients. It involves combining, mixing, or altering ingredients to create a medication tailored to meet the unique needs of an individual patient when commercially available medications don't fit their requirements.

You might need compounding if you have allergies to ingredients in commercial medications, need a different strength than available, require a different dosage form (like liquid instead of pills), need multiple medications combined, or if your medication has been discontinued by the manufacturer.

Compounded medications are regulated by state boards of pharmacy rather than the FDA. However, we use only FDA-approved ingredients and follow strict quality standards. Our compounding practices comply with USP (United States Pharmacopeia) guidelines and Maryland Board of Pharmacy regulations.
